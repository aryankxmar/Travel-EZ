About Ajanta and Ellora Caves

Ajanta and Ellora Caves are a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in the Aurangabad district of Maharashtra, India. These caves are renowned for their ancient rock-cut architecture and intricate sculptures.

Historical Significance

The Ajanta Caves date back to the 2nd century BCE and served as a center for Buddhist religious art and ritual. The Ellora Caves span a period from the 6th to 10th centuries CE and showcase a fusion of Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist cultures.

Main Attractions

  • Ajanta Caves: Magnificent Buddhist cave temples featuring intricate paintings and sculptures depicting the life of Buddha.
  • Ellora Caves: Impressive rock-cut temples representing Hindu, Jain, and Buddhist architecture, including the famous Kailasa Temple.

Local Cuisine

Indulge in the flavorful cuisine of Maharashtra during your visit:

  • Vegetarian Thali: A traditional meal consisting of various vegetarian dishes served with rice and bread.
  • Mutton Kolhapuri: Spicy mutton curry cooked with authentic Kolhapuri spices.
  • Puran Poli: Sweet flatbread stuffed with a mixture of jaggery and lentils, served as a dessert.
