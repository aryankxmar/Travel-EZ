About Masrur Temple

Masrur Temple, also known as the Masrur Rock-cut Temple, is a group of rock-cut temples located in the Kangra Valley of Himachal Pradesh, India. The temple complex is renowned for its remarkable monolithic rock-cut architecture.

Historical Significance

The Masrur Temple complex dates back to the 8th century AD and is believed to have been constructed by the rulers of the Katoch dynasty. The temple architecture bears resemblance to the Dravidian and Nagara styles of temple architecture.

Main Attractions

  • Temple Architecture: Marvel at the intricate carvings and sculptures adorning the temple walls and pillars.
  • Shiva Temple: The central shrine dedicated to Lord Shiva is the main attraction of the complex.
  • Surrounding Landscape: Enjoy panoramic views of the picturesque Kangra Valley from the temple complex.

Local Cuisine

Indulge in the delicious cuisine of Himachal Pradesh during your visit:

  • Dham: A traditional feast comprising rice, dal, rajma, curd, and sweet rice served on a leaf plate.
  • Siddu: Steamed bread stuffed with poppy seeds, dry fruits, and spices, served with ghee.
  • Khatta: A tangy curry made with tamarind, jaggery, and spices, typically served with rice.

Accommodation Options

Experience comfortable stays in the Kangra Valley:

  • The Exotica Resort: A luxury resort offering modern amenities and breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains.
  • Hotel The Judges Court: A heritage hotel set amidst lush greenery, providing a peaceful retreat for travelers.
  • Guesthouses and Homestays: Explore budget-friendly accommodation options available in nearby villages.
