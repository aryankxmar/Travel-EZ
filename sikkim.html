About the Trip

Sikkim, nestled in the eastern Himalayas, captivates with its stunning landscapes, including lush valleys and snow-capped peaks, offering a blend of natural beauty and rich cultural heritage.

Trip Highlights

  • Gangtok: Explore the charming capital city, known for its Buddhist pilgrimage sites, bustling markets, and breathtaking views of the Kanchenjunga mountain range.
  • Tsomgo Lake:Visit this serene glacial lake located at an altitude of 3,753 meters, surrounded by steep mountains and offering opportunities for yak rides and scenic walks.
  • Nathu La Pass: Embark on a journey to this historic mountain pass on the Indo-China border, known for its strategic significance and panoramic views of the Himalayas.
  • Yuksom: Discover the historic town of Yuksom, the first capital of Sikkim, and embark on treks to nearby attractions like Dubdi Monastery and Khecheopalri Lake.
  • Pelling: Enjoy stunning views of the Himalayas, including the majestic Mt. Khangchendzonga, from this picturesque hill station, known for its tranquility and natural beauty.

Places to Visit

  • Gangtok

    Gangtok, the capital city of Sikkim, is nestled in the eastern Himalayan range at an altitude of 1,650 meters (5,410 feet). It is known for its stunning natural beauty, rich culture, and vibrant atmosphere. Here is some information about Gangtok:

    • Scenic Beauty: Gangtok offers breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, lush green valleys, and cascading waterfalls. The city's landscape is adorned with colorful flowers and dense forests, making it a paradise for nature lovers.
    • Cultural Diversity: Gangtok is home to a diverse population comprising Nepali, Bhutia, and Lepcha communities, among others. This cultural amalgamation is reflected in the city's architecture, cuisine, and festivals, making it a melting pot of traditions and customs.
    • Adventure Activities: Gangtok is a hub for adventure enthusiasts, offering a wide range of activities such as trekking, river rafting, paragliding, and mountain biking. Popular trekking destinations include Dzongri Trek, Goechala Trek, and Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek.
    • Shopping: Visitors can indulge in shopping at various markets and boutiques in Gangtok, where they can find traditional Sikkimese handicrafts, woolen garments, Tibetan artifacts, and souvenirs.
    • Cuisine: Gangtok offers a diverse culinary experience, with a mix of Tibetan, Nepali, and Sikkimese cuisines. Visitors can savor local delicacies such as momos, thukpa, gundruk, and traditional Sikkimese dishes like Phagshapa and Chhurpi Soup
    • Accessibility:Gangtok is well-connected by road and air. The nearest airport is Bagdogra Airport in West Bengal, located approximately 125 kilometers away, while the nearest railway station is New Jalpaiguri (NJP) Railway Station, approximately 148 kilometers away. Regular buses and taxis ply between Gangtok and nearby cities.

  • Tsomgo Lake

    Natural Beauty:Tsomgo Lake is renowned for its breathtaking natural beauty, surrounded by steep snow-capped mountains and alpine forests.

    Glacial Origin: The lake is formed by the melting of glaciers and is considered sacred by the local Sikkimese people. It remains frozen during the winter months and offers stunning views of snow-covered landscapes.

    Spiritual Significance:Tsomgo Lake holds significant spiritual importance for the local Buddhist and Hindu communities. It is believed to be a sacred place blessed by deities and is often visited by pilgrims who offer prayers and perform rituals on its shores.

    Flora and Fauna:The area surrounding Tsomgo Lake is rich in biodiversity, with diverse flora and fauna. Visitors can spot various species of migratory birds, including Brahminy ducks and red pandas, amidst the pristine natural surroundings.

    Accessibility:Tsomgo Lake is located approximately 40 kilometers from the capital city of Gangtok and is accessible via a scenic drive through winding mountain roads. Due to its high altitude and weather conditions, the lake remains accessible only during certain months of the year, typically from May to November.

    Permit Requirement:Visitors need to obtain a permit from the Sikkim Tourism Department to visit Tsomgo Lake, as it is located in a restricted area near the Indo-China border. The permit can be obtained through registered tour operators or directly from the tourism office in Gangtok.

    Adventure Activities: Apart from enjoying the stunning views of the lake and surrounding landscapes, visitors can also indulge in activities like yak rides, snow tubing, and photography. During the winter months, the frozen lake offers opportunities for ice skating and snowball fights.

    • Nathu La Pass

      Nathu La Pass is a high-altitude mountain pass located in the Indian state of Sikkim, near the border with Tibet Autonomous Region of China. Here is some information about Nathu La Pass:

      Geographical Location:Nathu La Pass is situated at an elevation of approximately 4,310 meters (14,140 feet) above sea level in the Eastern Himalayas. It serves as a strategic passage connecting the Indian state of Sikkim with Tibet Autonomous Region of China.

      Historical Significance: Historically, Nathu La Pass has been an important trade route between India and Tibet, facilitating trade and cultural exchange between the two regions. It was reopened in 2006 for border trade after being closed for several decades.

      Strategic Importance: Due to its strategic location near the border with China, Nathu La Pass holds significant strategic importance for both India and China. It was the site of a border conflict between the two countries in 1967 and has since been closely monitored by both sides.

      Tourist Attraction:In addition to its historical and strategic significance, Nathu La Pass has become a popular tourist destination in Sikkim. Visitors can enjoy breathtaking views of the surrounding Himalayan mountains and explore the nearby attractions, including Tsomgo Lake and Baba Harbhajan Singh Temple.

      Border Trade:Nathu La Pass serves as a major border trading point between India and China. It is one of the key border trading posts where traders from both countries engage in bilateral trade of goods and commodities.

    Activities

    Sikkim offers a wide range of activities for travelers to enjoy its stunning landscapes, rich culture, and adventurous spirit. Here are some popular activities to do in Sikkim:

    • Trekking: Sikkim is a trekker's paradise, with numerous trails offering varying levels of difficulty. Trekking routes like the Goecha La Trek, Dzongri Trek, and Kanchenjunga Base Camp Trek provide breathtaking views of snow-capped mountains, lush valleys, and pristine lakes.
    • River Rafting:Adventure enthusiasts can enjoy river rafting in the Teesta and Rangit rivers, which offer thrilling rapids against the backdrop of picturesque scenery. The Teesta River is especially popular for its challenging rapids and scenic beauty.
    • Mountain BikingExplore the rugged terrain of Sikkim on a mountain biking adventure. Ride through winding mountain roads, lush forests, and quaint villages while enjoying panoramic views of the Himalayas.
    • Cultural Tours: Immerse yourself in Sikkim's rich cultural heritage by visiting its monasteries, temples, and traditional villages. Explore places like Rumtek Monastery, Pemayangtse Monastery, and Yuksom to learn about Buddhist culture and history.
    • Hot Springs:Relax and rejuvenate in Sikkim's natural hot springs, known for their therapeutic properties. Visit places like Yumthang Valley, Yumey Samdong, and Reshi Hot Springs to soak in the warm mineral waters amidst scenic surroundings.
    • Paragliding:Soar high above the mountains and valleys of Sikkim on a paragliding adventure. Enjoy bird's-eye views of the picturesque landscapes while experiencing the thrill of flying like a bird.
