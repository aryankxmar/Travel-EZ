About Konark Sun Temple

The Konark Sun Temple, also known as the Black Pagoda, is a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Konark, Odisha, India. It is dedicated to the Hindu Sun God, Surya, and is renowned for its impressive architecture and intricate stone carvings.

Historical Significance

The Konark Sun Temple was built in the 13th century by King Narasimhadeva I of the Eastern Ganga Dynasty. The temple served as a monumental representation of the chariot of the Sun God, with 24 elaborately carved stone wheels and exquisite sculptures depicting various aspects of life.

Main Attractions

  • Chariot Structure: Admire the impressive architectural design of the temple resembling the chariot of Surya.
  • Stone Carvings: Explore the intricate stone carvings depicting celestial beings, animals, and mythological scenes.
  • Natyamandir: Visit the Natyamandir (dance hall) known for its beautiful sculptures and intricate artwork.

Local Cuisine

Indulge in the delicious cuisine of Odisha during your visit:

  • Pakhala Bhata: Fermented rice soaked in water served with accompaniments like fried vegetables, fish, or curries.
  • Chenna Poda: A traditional Odia dessert made with cottage cheese, sugar, and cardamom, baked to perfection.
  • Chingudi Jhola: A spicy shrimp curry cooked with tomatoes, onions, and traditional Odia spices.

Accommodation Options
